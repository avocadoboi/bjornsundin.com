This is a simple demo of the discrete fourier transform. The signal on the left is projected onto a set of orthogonal complex exponential basis vectors, splitting it up into its frequency components.

Drag to edit a signal, and the other will update. Hold shift to set samples to zero. Only half of the frequencies are shown, because the other half is symmetrical since frequencies over pi appear to move the other direction when sampled like this. The time domain signal and the frequency phase signals are bipolar (phase is [-pi, pi]) while the frequency amplitude signal is unipolar (it consists of the absolute values of the complex coefficients)
